Nestled high in the Andes Mountains in southeastern Peru, Cusco is rich with history. Cusco was the historic capital of the Inca Empire from the 13th to the 16th century and is declared a World Heritage Site by UNESCO. As the location of the Secondary Cities Pilot project, the proposal is to build local capacity in geospatial science through a long-term engagement in the community. Cusco is a dynamic environment facing risks including flooding and landslides. Secondary Cities is promoting geospatial technologies and map making to address essential planning issues through partnering with the National University of Saint Anthony the Abbot of Cusco, the Office of the Geographer, the US Embassy, and ESRI.
